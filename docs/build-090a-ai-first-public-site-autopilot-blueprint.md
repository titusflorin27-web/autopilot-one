# Build #090A — AI-First Public Site & Autopilot Blueprint

**Project:** AUTOPILOT-TITUS / Autopilot One  
**Status:** strategic blueprint for public site redesign and 95% AI automation  
**Date:** 2026-07-09  
**Scope:** save and formalize the agreed direction before implementation  
**Runtime behavior changed:** no  
**Stripe live enabled:** no  
**Tracking added:** no  
**Public site copied from competitors:** no

---

## 1. Core decision

Autopilot One will move toward a competitive public site inspired by the structure and conversion logic of strong AI chat/customer support products, while staying original in copy, design, positioning and implementation.

The strategic reference products are:

- Tidio — closer to small and medium businesses, live chat, AI agent, widget, lead capture, help desk and automations;
- Intercom — premium/enterprise benchmark for AI support, live chat, inbox, help desk and customer operations.

Autopilot One must not copy either product. The goal is to use the market pattern and build an original, controlled, AI-first platform for SMEs.

---

## 2. Final product goal

The final product goal is:

```text
AI executes.
Human approves.
System logs.
Nothing critical happens hidden.
```

Target automation level:

```text
approximately 95% AI-assisted automation
```

This means AI should execute most operational work, while the founder or client approves critical gates.

## 3. Positioning

### 3.1 Tidio-style market pattern

Tidio positions around:

- AI agent;
- live chat/widget;
- help desk/inbox;
- flows/automations;
- integrations;
- customer support and conversion.

### 3.2 Autopilot One positioning

Autopilot One will position as:

```text
AI Agent + CRM Lite for SMEs that want fast website replies, lead capture, guided onboarding and controlled automation.
```

The local strategic angle is:

```text
A simpler, more controlled AI agent and lead-capture operating system for SMEs in Romania and Europe.
```

## 4. Differentiation

Autopilot One is not a Tidio clone.

The differentiators are:

1. demo-first activation;
2. controlled commercial onboarding;
3. website analysis before recommendation;
4. AI plan recommendation;
5. CRM Lite connected to conversations, leads, tasks and notifications;
6. guided knowledge-base preparation;
7. AI go-live readiness check;
8. human approval gates;
9. no hidden automatic billing or overage charging;
10. founder/internal operations manual and audit discipline.

The system must require human approval for:

1. final commercial offer for a new client;
2. paid plan activation;
3. Stripe/live billing changes;
4. go-live on a client website;
5. Business custom contracts;
6. legal/GDPR-sensitive replies;

9. mass email sending;
